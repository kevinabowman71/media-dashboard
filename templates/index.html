📊 Media Analysis Guide

😊 Sentiment

Indicates whether the headline tone is Positive, Neutral, or Negative.

🔥 Intensity

Measures emotional strength. High intensity headlines use dramatic or emotionally charged language.

🛡 Credibility

A 1–10 source trust score based on journalistic reliability and editorial standards.

🔎 Cross-Confirmation

Shows whether multiple outlets are reporting similar versions of the same story.

⚠ Framing Risk

Indicates how strongly the headline may be emotionally framed or rhetorically shaped.

Narrative Analysis Tools
🚀 Narrative Momentum

Narrative Momentum highlights stories that are spreading rapidly across multiple news outlets.

The system measures how many sources are reporting a similar story and how quickly those reports appear over time.

Stories with higher momentum are gaining coverage quickly across the media ecosystem and may represent emerging or rapidly developing news narratives.

The display shows how many outlets reported the story and the time span between the earliest and latest coverage detected.

Higher momentum often indicates a story that is quickly gaining media attention.

🔥 Top Narratives Right Now

Identifies the biggest news stories currently appearing across multiple outlets. The system groups similar headlines from different sources into clusters and ranks them by how many outlets are covering the same story.

A higher source count indicates a story that is gaining wider media attention.

🧭 Narrative Timeline

Shows how a news narrative spreads across different parts of the media ecosystem. Each bar represents when outlets with different editorial leanings first reported the story.

Left, Center, and Right labels indicate ideological media groups. Shorter bars represent earlier coverage, while longer bars indicate outlets that reported the story later.

🧭 Narrative Polarization

Narrative Polarization measures how a news story is distributed across different ideological segments of the media ecosystem.

Each narrative cluster is analyzed to determine how many outlets with Left, Center, and Right editorial leanings are reporting the story.

Large differences between groups may indicate that a narrative is being amplified more strongly within one part of the media landscape.

🌍 Narrative Diversity

Narrative Diversity measures how evenly a story is being reported across different parts of the media ecosystem.

The system compares how many outlets with Left, Center, and Right editorial leanings are covering the same narrative cluster.

A higher diversity score indicates that the story is appearing across a broad range of media perspectives. Lower scores suggest that coverage is concentrated within a smaller segment of the media landscape.

This metric can help identify narratives that are widely shared versus those that may be circulating primarily within one media ecosystem.

Score Interpretation 0.00 completely concentrated (one ecosystem only) 0.20–0.35 low diversity 0.35–0.55 moderate diversity 0.55–0.75 high diversity 0.75+ very balanced coverage

🗺 Narrative Map

The Narrative Map visualizes how a story moves across the media ecosystem.

It displays the relative presence of coverage among Left, Center, and Right media outlets using directional arrows.

This helps illustrate whether a narrative is concentrated in one media segment or spreading across multiple editorial environments.

🌐 Narrative Ecosystem Graph

The Narrative Ecosystem Graph provides a visual summary of how a major narrative is distributed across different media ecosystems.

The chart compares the number of outlets reporting the story within Left, Center, and Right editorial groups.

This visualization helps reveal whether a narrative is broadly shared across the media landscape or concentrated within specific ideological communities.
